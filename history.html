SAINT LOUIS IX

The Sainte-Chapelle in Paris was built in the mid-13th century by King Louis IX (future Saint-Louis) to house his collection of religious relics. The Sainte-Chapelle is considered among the highest achievements of the Rayonnant period of Gothic architecture. It was commissioned by King Louis IX of France to house his collection of Passion relics, including Christ's Crown of Thorns.
